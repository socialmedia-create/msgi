Counselling

  1. Home
  2. Counselling

Counselling can provide numerous benefits to engineering college students, helping them to navigate challenges during their academic journey and beyond. It offers academic support, career guidance, and emotional well-being aid. Students also develop soft skills and improve decision-making.

Counselling & Grievance Redressal

Supporting students in academic, personal, and career development with care and integrity

🎯 Role of Counselling

The emerging role of counselling involves helping students to complete their academic objectives; the reduction of student attrition is a priority. Counselors must perform the roles of student developers and guides for learning. They communicate the importance of skill building and other academic requirements, encouraging students to build a pattern of success. Essential qualities include professional mission, rapport, and empathy.

📝 Responsibility

As student developers, counselors should emphasize academics realistically and vividly. They are responsible for providing counseling, information, and support services to help students make informed educational and career choices. Development includes social, intellectual, psychological, and ethical dimensions.

📌 Counselor Services

  • Assessing student ability using placement tests and interest inventories
  • Clarifying education and career goals
  • Helping students utilize college resources

Counselling Methods & Techniques

Students are guided through various evidence-based therapy approaches including:

  • Psychotherapy
  • Transactional Analysis
  • Psychodrama
  • JPMR
  • Grounding techniques for focus & anxiety
  • Body Relaxation – Breathing Techniques
  • Physical Exercise – Yoga & Workouts
  • Meditation
  • Talk Therapy

Academic Progress Monitoring

Students' academic improvement is monitored regularly and visually represented as:

  • Red – Starting / Needs Guidance
  • Yellow – Slight Improvement
  • Green – Full Improvement

Progress is followed up weekly by the counselors.
