ABOUT US

About the Conference

ICTAGI 2026 (International Conference on Transformative AI for Global Impact) is a premier gathering of researchers, practitioners, and industry experts. The conference focuses on exploring how Artificial Intelligence transforms industries, society, and sustainability.

The conference will be held at Meenakshi Sundararajan Engineering College, Chennai, India from August 12th – 14th, 2026. It provides an interdisciplinary platform to discuss recent innovations, trends, challenges, and solutions in Artificial Intelligence and its global impact.

ABOUT MSEC

About the Institution

Meenakshi Sundararajan Engineering College (MSEC) was established by the IIET Society in 2001. The institution is a part of the prestigious KRS Group of Institutions, which also includes the renowned Indian Institute of Engineering Technology (IIET) established in 1947 by our Founder Late Shri K. R. Sundararajan, Meenakshi College for Women, and the Meenakshi Sundararajan School of Management.

The institutions on the KRS Campus are known for the quality of education they impart and their stringent levels of discipline. MSEC has consistently outshone its peers not only in academics, but also in co-curricular activities.

MSEC is committed to imparting engineering education to young men and women, grooming their overall personality with the highest emphasis on ethical values, and honing them to face the challenges of the industry and the nation at large.

ABOUT CHENNAI

Chennai, formerly Madras, is the capital city of Tamil Nadu, India. Located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal, it is one of the largest cultural, economic, and educational centers of South India.

Known as the “Gateway to South India”, Chennai is famous for its rich heritage, classical music, dance forms, architectural landmarks, and delicious cuisine. The city blends tradition with modern development, offering visitors a vibrant cultural experience.

Marina Beach

Iconic coastline of Chennai, one of the longest urban beaches in the world.

Kapaleeshwarar Temple

Historic Dravidian architecture dedicated to Lord Shiva in Mylapore.

Santhome Basilica

Major Christian landmark built over the tomb of St. Thomas.

Modern Chennai Skyline

A growing metro hub showcasing the city's development.

Ripon Building

Iconic Indo-Saracenic landmark and Chennai Corporation headquarters.

Valluvar Kottam

Monument honoring the great Tamil poet Thiruvalluvar.

Vandalur Zoo

One of India's largest zoological parks with diverse wildlife.

Mahabalipuram

UNESCO World Heritage site known for ancient rock-cut temples.

Sharadambal Temple

Dedicated to Goddess Sharadambal in varying architectural styles.
